Tomas Diez

I am a passionate founder working at the intersection of technology and sustainable urbanism for over 15 years. My work focuses on solving critical problems facing communities around the world by building networks for local production and citizen empowerment. This has taken the form of pioneering digital fabrication laboratories, from the globally recognized Fab Lab Barcelona to launching new innovation hubs like Fab Lab Bali.

Key Projects

Co-Founder, Fab Lab Barcelona

fablabbcn.org

A pioneering digital fabrication laboratory in Barcelona, one of the leading nodes in the global Fab Lab network, serving as a center for research, education, and innovation.

Problem it aims to solve:

It addresses the need for accessible, hands-on education in digital technologies and provides a space for citizens and entrepreneurs to prototype ideas, fostering local innovation and a skilled workforce for the future of manufacturing.

Co-Founder, Fab City Global Initiative

fab.city

A global network of cities and regions committed to producing nearly everything they consume by 2054, transforming how we source and use materials.

Problem it aims to solve:

It directly challenges our current wasteful and fragile global supply chain. By bringing production back to a local level, the initiative aims to build urban resilience, create circular economies, and drastically reduce the environmental footprint of cities.

Co-Founder, Smart Citizen

smartcitizen.me

An open-source platform combining hardware sensors and software that allows everyday people to measure and collect data about their environment, like air quality and noise pollution.

Problem it aims to solve:

A lack of accessible, granular, and citizen-owned data about our cities. It empowers communities with the evidence needed to understand their surroundings, advocate for healthier environments, and participate in urban planning.

Co-Founder, Fablabs.io

fablabs.io

The official online platform and directory that connects the entire international network of thousands of Fab Labs.

Problem it aims to solve:

As the Fab Lab network grew exponentially, there was no central hub for labs to connect, share knowledge, or for people to find them. The platform solves this fragmentation, fostering a cohesive and collaborative global community that can learn from each other.
